Music

Ian Chan is a Hong Kong-born Canadian composer whose work integrates and synthesizes his diverse interests in contemporary classical music, jazz, and musical theatre. Ian's compositions have been performed across Canada and the United States by ensembles including the Harvard-Radcliffe Collegium Musicum, Sneak Peek Orchestra, Etobicoke Philharmonic Orchestra, and Scarborough Philharmonic Orchestra, the latter two of which named him their 2017 Promising Young Composer and 2018 Emerging Young Composer, respectively. Ian's work has been widely sponsored at Harvard University, where he has written for the Harvard Choruses New Music Initiative, the Office of the Arts, and the Department of Music, as well as for collaborations with the Boston Conservatory at Berklee College of Music. Ian has studied with Kevin Lau, and has been mentored by Robert Kyr, Christos Hatzis, Hing-Yan Chan, and Ronald Royer. A list of Ian's compositions can be found below.

Beyond composition, Ian's musical interests are widespread. As a conductor, Ian has experience leading orchestras, pit orchestras, wind ensembles, and choirs; in the 2020-2021 season he will be serving as conductor of the American Repertory Theater's Family Show and the Harvard College Opera's assistant music director and chorusmaster. As a vocalist, Ian sings regularly with the Din and Tonics, a semi-professional jazz a cappella group, and his choir, the Harvard-Radcliffe Collegium Musicum. Ian's instrumental work has awarded him First Prize at the Canadian Music Competition in piano and Associate Diplomas from the Royal Conservatory of Music in both piano and violin. Recordings of Ian's work as a conductor, singer, pianist, violinist, and percussionist are available on request.

Ian firmly believes in music as both a bridge between communities and a creator of community. He is fascinated by the world's musical potential, and strives continually to explore and develop artistic representations of global themes, ideas, and stories. Ian loves collaborating with fellow creators to produce and inspire exciting, new art.

If you're interested in working with Ian, or if you'd like to learn more about his current projects, don't hesitate to email him at ianchan [at] college.harvard.edu.
